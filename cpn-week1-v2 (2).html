Week 1 · The Pledge

Don't make
things worse.

Try one practice today. Use a drill when you need it. Log one moment.

This week, you are working on catching yourself before you react.

How you treat people stays with you. A careless text, a cheap shot at someone vulnerable, or a lie to avoid conflict often creates more suffering than it prevents. This week is about noticing that connection clearly.

Morning Intention
"Today I will try not to cause harm to myself or anyone around me."
Today's Practices
  • Notice one moment today when you felt the urge to be reactive, shut down, or avoid. Just notice it. Don't change it.
  • If someone's name comes up in a conversation that turns negative, notice the pull before you join in.
  • Eat one meal without your phone today. Notice the difference between being present and being distracted.
11am and 3pm Check-in
Prompt 1Did I say or do anything this morning that made things worse for me or someone else?
Prompt 2Was there a moment I could have paused, but didn’t?
Drills
In the moment
One Second
You are about to say it, send it, or do it. Pause.
  • 1Press your feet into the floor.
  • 2Notice where the urge is sitting. Chest, jaw, hands?
  • 3Breathe out slowly, once.
  • 4Ask: will this make things better or worse?
  • 5Then decide.
The gap between impulse and action is where you have power.
End of day
How'd That Land
One interaction from today. Pick one.
  • 1Picture it.
  • 2Notice how it feels to remember it. Does anything feel off?
  • 3If it feels off, name it plainly: "that wasn't kind."
  • 4No guilt. Just see it clearly.
Seeing it clearly changes how you act moment to moment.
